You will be working with two systems for this exam.
One system is the physical system at which you are seated. 
The other system is a virtual system which is hosted on your physical system and
has been pre-installed with Red Hat Enterprise Linux. 
Unless otherwise specified, all exam items are to be performed on the virtual
system only.You do not have root access to the physical host system
but you may use it as necessary to test services that you may be
required to configure on your virtual system. 
Before you may proceed with the main part of the exam, you must
complete the tasks outlined in the link below.
Be aware that you are not permitted to communicate with other
examinees during the course of this exam.  You are also prohibited
from connecting to the systems of other examinees.  The testing system
and the network will be monitored, and misuse of either will result
in a grade of zero on the exam.

	During the exam, you may shutdown or reboot your virtual system
		at any time.  You may do this from the virtual system itself
		or you can control the virtual system from your physical system.

		To access or control your exam system from your physical system,
		click on the 'VM Control' icon on your desktop.  This will present a
		menu with four options:

			Connect to Exam VM Console - this will open a window that
			connects to the console of your exam system.

			Start Exam VM - this will start your exam virtual
			system if it is not running.  If your system is already
			running this has no effect.

			Shutdown Exam VM - this will do a graceful shut down of your
			exam virtual system.

			Power Off Exam VM - this will immediately shut down your
			exam virtual system.

			Exam VM Console - this will open a window that
			connects to the console of your exam system.
